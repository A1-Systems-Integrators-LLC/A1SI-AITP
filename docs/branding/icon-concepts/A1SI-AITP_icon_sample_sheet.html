Decision — June 2026

C8 "Hex Trade Node" was selected and shipped. AITP adopts the A1SI hexagon badge so it reads as a visible sibling of CVWS / TERM / WIFI on a home screen, with the rising-candlestick glyph carrying the trading story inside. The full family (web favicons, iOS AppIcon set, Android mipmaps + Play-Store art, and the live PWA set + site.webmanifest) is generated by export_icons.py; reference copies and the QA sheet live in ../icons/. The remaining 11 concepts stay here as the design record.

Design & Marketing recommendation (pre-decision)

AITP currently ships only a loose favicon.png / apple-touch-icon.png and is not part of the house family. Every concept above uses the A1SI house construction — gradient navy squircle + bold solid white glyph + a single glowing cyan accent — shared with A1SI-CVWS, A1SI-TERM and A1SI-WIFI, so AITP joins them as a visible sibling. No green/red: the rising-trend story rides on candle height and the cyan glow.

Primary recommendation: A1 "Candlestick Rise." The universal "this is a markets / trading app" mark — zero re-learning, maximum legibility, and it drops straight into the light/dark/tinted export pipeline. The safe default.

Differentiation pick: C7 "AI Pulse." If the icon should say what makes AITP different — AI-driven, automated, live — the signal fan rising off the candles carries that, and it is the tightest tie to the A1SI house motif.

Family pick: C8 "Hex Trade Node." CVWS shipped a Hex Node and TERM explored one; adopting the hexagon badge here makes the apps visible siblings on a home screen, with only the central glyph changing per product.

Ownable pick: D10 "A Monogram" / D11 "Up Arrow Glyph." The most trademark-friendly routes, and D11 doubles as the cleanest small-size favicon regardless of which app icon wins.

Next step: select 1–2 directions. We then run export_icons.py on the chosen SVG to produce the full export set (dashboard favicons, iOS/Android app icons incl. dark + tinted variants, store art), with reference copies landing in ../icons/ before it replaces the shipped favicon.
